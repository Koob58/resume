In Short

Making one's vision of a web application come alive is never easy. It requires a lot of balanced thinking. Fresh versus time-tested, DRY versus flexible, reasonably optimized versus prematurely optimized. Designing an app is as much a scientific as it is a creative process, requiring a "feel" for it.

I have worked on numerous projects, some of which are working autonomously for years, while others I constantly maintain. Some examples are Unpublik, Printio, Scripteka, and WeHike. I should note that these are my own non-profit as well as commercial endeavors, not my contracting work. The only thing I find more fun than programming is programming with a higher purpose.

I strive for fun and enjoyable partnership. Albeit the effort is subjective, there are two things I know that help establish this kind of relationship. Firstly it's delivering expected results. Secondly, it's finding pleasure in teaching and learning. I try to find time to blog about things I do. This way I get my ideas criticized and condemned, or praised and adapted. Feedback is the main ingredient in balanced thinking as it provides a much needed reality check.

Philosophy

I am a minimalist, enjoying simplicity, and clarity, as exemplified in this resume. In Rails I tend to follow REST and TDD principles, yet I never stay blindly hooked on paradigms. There are always exceptions. I love clean code and refactoring (and I read both of these books). I've been extensively exploring architecture and design patterns, and over the years realized that a simple architecture is more valuable and harder to produce than a complex one.
